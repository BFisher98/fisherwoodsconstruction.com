How Much Does a Basement Finish Cost in Utah?

Finishing a basement is one of the smartest investments a Utah homeowner can make. It adds livable square footage, increases your home's resale value, and gives your family more room to spread out — all without the cost of building an addition.

But one of the most common questions we get is: how much is this actually going to cost me? Here's an honest answer.

Average Basement Finish Costs in Utah

In Utah County and Salt Lake County, you can expect to pay roughly:

  • Basic finish (framing, drywall, flooring, basic bathroom): $25–$40 per square foot
  • Mid-range finish (custom trim, better fixtures, full bathroom, egress windows): $40–$65 per square foot
  • High-end finish (wet bar, home theater, custom built-ins, tile work): $65–$100+ per square foot

So for a typical 1,000 sq ft basement, you're looking at anywhere from $25,000 to $65,000 depending on the scope and finishes you choose.

What Affects the Price Most?

Several factors will move your number up or down:

  • Bathroom additions — Adding a bathroom is one of the biggest cost drivers. Rough plumbing, tile, fixtures, and a toilet/vanity can add $8,000–$15,000 alone.
  • Egress windows — Required by Utah code if you're adding a bedroom. Budget $1,500–$3,000 per window.
  • Ceiling height — Low ceilings require more creative solutions and can limit what you can do.
  • Existing rough-ins — If your builder already stubbed in plumbing, you'll save significantly on a bathroom.
  • Finish level — LVP flooring vs. hardwood, basic doors vs. custom trim — these choices add up fast.

What's Typically Included in a Basement Finish?

  • Framing and insulation
  • Drywall and paint
  • Flooring (LVP, carpet, or tile)
  • Lighting and electrical
  • Doors and trim
  • HVAC extensions
  • Bathroom (if included in scope)

How Long Does It Take?

A standard basement finish in Utah typically takes 6–12 weeks from permit approval to final walkthrough, depending on scope and contractor schedule. Permitting alone can take 2–4 weeks in most Utah County cities.

Tips for Getting the Best Value

  • Get at least 2–3 bids and compare them line by line, not just total price
  • Ask your contractor what's included and what's excluded — surprises usually come from unclear scopes
  • Don't skip the permit — unpermitted work can cause serious problems when you sell
  • Prioritize the bathroom rough-in if you don't have one — it's much cheaper to do it now than later
