How Much Does Interior Trim Work Cost in Utah?

Trim work is one of those things that most people don't notice when it's done well — but everyone notices when it's done poorly or missing altogether. Good trim elevates a space from feeling like a construction site to feeling like a finished home. Here's what it costs in Utah.

Types of Interior Trim Work

  • Baseboards — Run along the bottom of walls where they meet the floor
  • Door and window casings — Frame the openings of doors and windows
  • Crown molding — Decorative trim where walls meet the ceiling
  • Chair rail — Horizontal trim running around the middle of a wall
  • Wainscoting — Paneling on the lower portion of walls
  • Built-in shelving and millwork — Custom woodwork like bookcases, mantels, and built-ins

Average Costs in Utah

  • Baseboards: $1.50–$4 per linear foot installed
  • Door casings: $75–$200 per door opening
  • Window casings: $50–$150 per window
  • Crown molding: $4–$15 per linear foot installed depending on complexity
  • Wainscoting: $10–$30 per square foot
  • Custom built-ins: $300–$1,000+ per linear foot depending on design

What Affects the Price?

  • Profile complexity — Simple flat baseboards cost less than ornate multi-piece profiles
  • Material — MDF is the most affordable; solid wood costs more but takes paint and stain beautifully
  • Ceiling height — Taller ceilings mean more linear footage and more difficult installation for crown molding
  • Condition of existing walls — Out-of-square walls and corners require more skilled fitting
  • Paint vs. stain — Painted trim hides imperfections better; stained trim requires more precision

Is Trim Work Worth It?

Absolutely. Trim work is one of the highest-ROI finish investments you can make. It makes builder-grade homes look custom, adds perceived value to any space, and is one of the first things buyers notice during a home showing. Even simple baseboards and door casings make a dramatic difference in how finished a room feels.

DIY vs. Hiring a Pro

Trim work looks deceptively simple but requires precise cuts, coping joints, and an eye for detail. Poor trim work stands out immediately — gaps, bad miters, and uneven reveals are hard to ignore. For anything beyond simple baseboard replacement, we recommend hiring an experienced finish carpenter.
