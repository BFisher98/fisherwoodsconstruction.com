What Is a Tenant Improvement and How Does It Work?

If you're leasing commercial space in Utah — whether it's an office, retail store, or warehouse — you've probably heard the term tenant improvement (TI). But what does it actually mean, and how does it work? Here's a straightforward breakdown.

What Is a Tenant Improvement?

A tenant improvement is any renovation or build-out done to a commercial space to make it suitable for a specific tenant's use. This could include:

  • Framing and building new walls or offices
  • Installing flooring, ceilings, and lighting
  • Adding bathrooms or break rooms
  • HVAC modifications
  • Electrical and data upgrades
  • Custom millwork, cabinetry, or reception areas
  • Exterior signage and storefront work

Who Pays for Tenant Improvements?

This is where it gets interesting — and negotiable. There are a few common arrangements:

  • Tenant Improvement Allowance (TIA) — The landlord gives the tenant a set dollar amount per square foot to spend on improvements. Anything over that amount is the tenant's responsibility.
  • Turnkey build-out — The landlord handles and pays for all improvements to the tenant's specifications.
  • Tenant-funded — The tenant pays for all improvements themselves, sometimes in exchange for lower rent or a longer lease term.

Always negotiate the TI allowance as part of your lease — it's one of the most valuable concessions a landlord can offer, especially in a softer market.

How Does the Process Work?

  1. Lease negotiation — Agree on the TI allowance and scope with your landlord
  2. Design and planning — Work with your contractor to develop a scope of work and floor plan
  3. Permitting — Most commercial TI work requires a building permit in Utah
  4. Construction — Your contractor completes the build-out
  5. Inspection and occupancy — City inspection and certificate of occupancy before you move in

How Much Do Tenant Improvements Cost in Utah?

Costs vary widely depending on the condition of the space and what's needed:

  • Basic office finish (paint, carpet, lighting): $20–$40 per square foot
  • Mid-range retail or office: $40–$75 per square foot
  • High-end or specialized space (medical, restaurant, custom): $75–$150+ per square foot

Tips for a Successful Tenant Improvement Project

  • Hire a licensed general contractor with commercial experience
  • Get the scope of work nailed down before construction starts — changes mid-project are expensive
  • Factor in permitting time when planning your move-in date
  • Understand what your landlord's approval process looks like before starting design
