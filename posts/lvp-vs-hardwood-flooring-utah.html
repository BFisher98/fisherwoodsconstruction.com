LVP vs Hardwood Flooring — Which Is Right for Your Utah Home?

Flooring is one of the biggest decisions in any remodel — and one of the most visible. Two of the most popular options right now are luxury vinyl plank (LVP) and hardwood. Both look great, but they're very different products. Here's an honest comparison to help you decide.

What Is LVP?

Luxury vinyl plank is a synthetic flooring product designed to look like wood. Modern LVP has come a long way — today's products have realistic wood grain textures, come in a wide range of styles, and are surprisingly durable. Brands like LifeProof, COREtec, and Shaw are popular choices.

Cost Comparison

  • LVP: $2–$7 per square foot for materials; $1–$3 per square foot for installation
  • Hardwood: $5–$15 per square foot for materials; $3–$8 per square foot for installation

On a 1,000 sq ft project, LVP might cost $5,000–$10,000 installed while hardwood could run $12,000–$23,000. That's a significant difference.

Durability in Utah's Climate

Utah's dry climate and temperature swings are actually a big factor here. Hardwood expands and contracts with humidity changes — in Utah's dry winters, this can cause gapping between boards. LVP is dimensionally stable and handles Utah's climate much better without warping or gapping.

Where Each Works Best

  • LVP: Basements, bathrooms, kitchens, laundry rooms, high-traffic areas, homes with pets or kids
  • Hardwood: Living rooms, dining rooms, bedrooms, spaces where you want maximum resale appeal

Note: Hardwood is not recommended for basements or below-grade spaces due to moisture issues. LVP is the go-to for basement finishes.

Maintenance

  • LVP: Sweep and mop — that's it. Highly scratch resistant and waterproof.
  • Hardwood: Can be refinished multiple times over its life, which is a big advantage. But it scratches more easily and cannot get wet.

Resale Value

Hardwood traditionally adds more resale value and is often what buyers expect in higher-end homes. However, high-quality LVP is increasingly accepted by buyers — especially in basements and mid-range homes. The gap in resale value is narrowing.

Our Honest Recommendation

For most Utah homeowners doing a remodel or basement finish, LVP is the smarter choice — it's more affordable, more durable in our climate, and looks excellent. For main living areas in higher-end homes where budget allows, hardwood is still hard to beat for warmth and long-term value.
